Delhi → Kathmandu → Pokhara → Delhi

Six days through the roof of the world

A culture, nature, and adventure itinerary for four — temples at dawn, peaks at sunrise, and a paraglide over Phewa Lake in between.

Duration
6 days
Group
4 travellers
Style
Mid-range comfort
Visa
Not required — Indian nationals
01
Delhi to Kathmandu
Arrival
Flight to Kathmandu
About 1.5 hours, several daily departures from Delhi
Check into a Thamel hotel
Mid-range base for the next two nights
Evening in Thamel
Markets, dinner with live Nepali folk music
02
Kathmandu valley heritage
Culture
Kathmandu Durbar Square
The old royal palace complex
Swayambhunath
The Monkey Temple, with views over the whole valley
Pashupatinath Temple
Nepal's most sacred Hindu site, on the Bagmati river
Boudhanath Stupa
A candlelit evening circuit around the world's largest stupa
03
Nagarkot sunrise, on to Pokhara
Nature
Early drive to Nagarkot
About 1.5 hrs — Himalayan range views, Everest on clear days
Flight to Pokhara
25 minutes — saves a full day versus the 6-7 hr drive
Phewa Lake, Lakeside
Evening stroll along the water
04
Pokhara nature and adventure
Adventure
Paragliding over Phewa Lake
Annapurna range as a backdrop — Pokhara's signature activity
World Peace Pagoda
By boat across the lake
Davis Falls and Gupteshwor Cave
Afternoon stop, close together
Lakeside evening
Cafes and bars along the water
05
Sarangkot sunrise, back to Kathmandu
Nature
Pre-dawn drive to Sarangkot
Sunrise over Annapurna and Machapuchare — one of Nepal's iconic views
Fly back to Kathmandu
Midday flight
Free afternoon
Thamel shopping, or an optional Trishuli River rafting trip
06
Bhaktapur and departure
Culture
Bhaktapur Durbar Square
About 45 min from Kathmandu — the best-preserved medieval Newari city
Pottery Square and juju dhau
Local crafts and the famous "king of yogurt"
Fly back to Delhi
Evening departure

Practical notes

Entry
No visa needed for Indian nationals — a valid photo ID is enough
Internal travel
Domestic flights (Kathmandu–Pokhara) recommended over the long drive
Currency
Nepali Rupee — Indian Rupees up to ₹100 notes are widely accepted
Best season
October–November and March–April for clear mountain views
Built for a group of four — adjust freely.
